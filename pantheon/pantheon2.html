Pantheon

Pantheon 2.0

Pantheon, the second edition of the inter institution competition by ICI CET Student's chapter was held on 18th March, 2016. Pantheon is a national level technical festival and is held in collaboration with the ICI Trivandrum Chapter. This fest provides an opportunity for to civil engineering students across the nation for showcasing their aptitude and skill in their field. The fest consists of five main events and an exhibition.

1. THE BEST ENGINEER

The main competition and the title event was “The Best Engineer” which sought for the contestant to go through a series of drilling rounds testing the participant's IQ, ability to think on their feet, stress handling capabilities and depth of subject knowledge. The competition consisted of a total of five rounds. The preliminary round was written test which had multiple choice type questions. The second round was group discussion on a socially relevant topic. The next round was a practical round to test the practical skills of the participants. The fourth round was a situation management round in which every participant was given a situation to solve. The final round was a quiz competition and based on the scores from the last four rounds.
The coordinators of this event were Sreejith K V and Ajay S R.

2. PAPER PRESENTATION

Paper Presentation was involved in Pantheon after the overwhelming response we got by conducting the competition twice for the students of the Chapter. The competition started days prior to the actual event with the participants sending in abstracts of their presentation. Based on these abstracts, a list of participants was selected to present their topics. The participants were given 10 minutes time limit to finish the presentation and 5 minutes time to answer to the questions by judges and the audience.
The coordinators of the event were Niranjana Warrier and Divya Chandran

3. SURVEY TECH HUNT

Survey Tech Hunt, the second event was a team competition. The idea was the same as that of a treasure hunt but with the difference being that the tasks consisted of both technical and non-technical questions. It consisted of three rounds- preliminary round of survey puzzle, second round to collect clues and third round to find the hidden treasure. The 1st 2nd and 3rd positions were declared based on the total points they earned.
The coordinators of the event were Malavika S and Arjun Nikhil

4. SITUATION MANAGEMENT

Situation Management was the fourth competition. The competition consisted of two rounds. The preliminary round was a screening round consisting of a written test. The second and final round had shortlisted teams competing each other to find an effective solution to a civil engineering problem of practical significance.
The coordinators of the event were Malavika Manoj and Vinaya Prakash.

5. WORKSHOPS

Two workshops were conducted in connection with Pantheon 2016. The workshop were conducted by DCS and BASF. DCS conducted a workshop on STAAD and BASF conducted a workshop on admixture.

STAAD WORKSHOP BY DCS

A class was conducted on basic STAAD by a team from DCS, which was a new venture separated from the InterCAD. About 35 participants registered for the event. It was held on 18th March from 10 am.

ADMIXTURE WORKSHOP BY BASF

The workshops feature was an overview of various admixtures for concreteand modified properties of concrete by using admixtures. It was held on 17th March 2016 at 10 am. Workshop was handled by the experienced faculties of BASF. ‘BASF-We create chemistry’ are the largest chemical producers in the world that is 150 year old headquartered in Germany. The Certificate was issued by Indian Concrete Institute, CET Chapter. Limited seats were there and about 35 students attended the workshop.

6. EXHIBITION

Models were exhibited by the members of our student chapter during the event. The exhibits were innovative and were improvised versions of existing ideas. The participants had to submit an abstract and had to present their exhibit to the judging panel. The coordinators of the event were Allan Chacko and Aminul Hassan. All the events were successfully conducted simultaneously and the winners were announced during the closing ceremony conducted in the CGPU hall in the evening and certificates and prize money were awarded to the winners. The chief guest of the event was Sri. Mir Mohammed Ali IAS, Project Director, Kerala Land Information Mission. A stall of the KLIM was put up in the college during Pantheon. ICI Trivandrum Chapter Chairman Sri. Madhu Mohan and Secretary Sri. Anwar Hussain were present in the occasion.

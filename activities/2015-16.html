1)INTRODUCTION TO REVIT AND STAAD

ICI CET Chapter organised an Introduction to Autodesk Revit on 12/8/2015 at 4.p.m in Seminar Hall 1, Civil Engineering Dept. The program was inaugurated by Dr. Mini. Soman, faculty-in-charge, ICI CET Chapter. The talk was conducted by InterCAD, a CADD software training institute. Mr. Shiyas Shafi, an official from InterCAD, gave an hour long introduction class and an onstage demonstration on Revit which was attended by over 40 students. He was accompanied by Mr. Vivek, another InterCAD official. Autodesk Revit is building information modelling software for architects, structural engineers, MEP engineers, designers and contractors. It allows users to design a building, structures and its components in 3D, annotate the model with 2D drafting elements, and access building information from the building model's database. A 20 hour programme which provides a platform for students to learn and get expertise was also planned to be conducted in the coming semester. In addition to Revit, InterCAD had also offered certified classes for STAAD and PRIMAVERA.

INDUSTRIAL VISITS

1)POABS M-SAND UNIT

ICI CET Chapter organized its first industrial visit for the academic year 2015-16 on 1st August 2015, Saturday to POABS M-Sand unit and Aruvikkara Water Treatment Plant. Over 30 student members from S5 and S3 batches attended the same. The group was accompanied by Prof. Biju.V, faculty, civil engineering department. At POAB M-Sand unit, students were able to see how M-sand (Manufactured Sand) is being produced on a large scale unit. M-Sand is usually used where there is a shortage of river sand. POAB also produced p-sand (plasticized sand) which is exclusively used for plastering. Crushed rocks which are transferred through conveyor belts are sieved through large mechanical sieves of various sizes such as 40mm, 20mm, 4.75mm, 0.3mm, 0.15mm etc. The aggregates sized between 4.75mm and 0.15mm is used for M-sand production. Finer aggregates are removed by adding water. Sieved aggregates are made into cubes using vertical shaft impact. Aggregates sized between 3mm and 0.15mm is used for p-sand production, which is directly used for plastering.

5)MATHUR AQUEDUCT

OCTOBER 18, 2015
As part of the ICI activities for this semester a group of 54 students made an industrial visit to a site of plinth lifting construction work, at Manacaud and Mathur aqueduct, Tamil Nadu. Mathur Aqueduct or Mathur Hanging Trough is an Aqueduct in Southern India, in Kanyakumari District of Tamil Nadu state. Built over the Pahrali River (also called Parazhiyar), it takes its name from Mathur, a hamlet near the Aqueduct, which is at a distance of about 3 kilometres from Thiruvattar town and about 60 km from Kanyakumari. It is one of the longest and highest aqueducts in South Asia and is also a popular tourist spot in Kanyakumari District.

ICI CET CHAPTER QUIZ

A technical quiz was conducted on 23rd July 2015, which was designed to test the technical knowledge and interest in the field of civil engineering. The event was open to all student members. The quiz was conducted in two stages. The preliminary stage was a written test, which was held on July 23rd, Wednesday. The test was based on basic civil engineering and constructions facts from around the globe. The test consisted of 20 questions for a duration of 20 minutes. More than 50 students participated in the prelims from which 5 teams of 2 members each were selected for the final stage. The finals were conducted on 24th July. The final round was exclusively based on wonders in civil engineering. There were four rounds in the final stage including those like identifying structures from given pictures and clues, identifying the relation between the pictures, identifying the eminent personalities in civil engineering field and other ordinary questions. The competition which raised the enthusiasm of the contestants to a whole new level saw the contestants fight till the very end. Winners of ICI Technical Quiz are:
